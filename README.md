## Project Overview
This project analyzes the health trends of high school students using the 2007 Youth Risk Behavior Survey (YRBS). We focus on both mental health (depressive symptoms) and physical health (BMI) to determine if there have been significant shifts compared to historical benchmarks.

## Research Question
* **Proportion Analysis:** Is the proportion of students who felt sad or hopeless for two weeks or more in 2007 different from the benchmark of 30% (p0=0.30)?
* **Mean Analysis:** Is the mean BMI percentile of students in 2007 different from the historical average of 65.0 (Ој0=65.0)?

## Dataset & Variables
* **Dataset:** `YRBS_2007.csv` (Total valid samples: **12,902**)
* **Selected Variables:**
    * `SadOrHopeless` (Categorical): Whether students felt sad or hopeless for 2+ weeks.
    * `BMIPCT` (Continuous): Body Mass Index percentile of the students.

## Benchmarks
* **Proportion Benchmark ($p_0$):** 0.285 (28.5%)
* **Mean Benchmark ($\mu_0$):** 65.0

## Key Findings
* **Depressive Symptoms:** The sample proportion was **29.78%** ($p < 0.05$). We found a statistically significant increase in students reporting depressive symptoms compared to 2005.
* **BMI Percentile:** The sample mean was **64.83** ($p = 0.4789$). There is no significant evidence to suggest that the mean BMI percentile has shifted from the historical average.

## Final Conclusion
Our analysis reveals a critical disconnect between physical and mental health trends. While adolescent **physical health (BMI) remained stable**, there was a **statistically significant decline in mental well-being** (increased depressive symptoms) in 2007. This suggests that school-based resources should prioritize psychological support and mental health interventions.
